Introduction

The course "Design and Analysis of Algorithms" explores advanced algorithms and data structures to solve computational problems efficiently. It emphasizes both the theoretical foundations and practical applications of algorithm design techniques.

  • Course Focus: Analyzing time and space efficiency, algorithm design strategies like Divide & Conquer, Dynamic Programming, and Greedy methods.
  • Studied Structures: Arrays, Trees, Graphs, Heaps, Tries, and Hash Maps.
  • Real-world Applications: From optimizing network flows to solving complex computational biology problems.

Course Project Introduction

Our project investigates optimizing city designs through algorithmic approaches. The problem space was identified by analyzing urban traffic flows and logistics systems, summarized in our white paper.

  • Problem Definition: Develop algorithms for efficient urban planning, focusing on traffic management and resource allocation.
  • Team Details:
    • Member 1: Traffic flow optimization
    • Member 2: Resource allocation and logistics
    • Member 3: City grid design for sustainability
  • Business Cases: Each member addresses a specific urban challenge, proposing algorithm-based solutions.

Course Learning Reflections

Algorithmic Concepts

Key reflections from the course:

  • Understanding the importance of iteration, recursion, and backtracking in solving complex problems.
  • The significance of space and time efficiency in practical scenarios, exploring orders of growth and complexity classes.
  • Insights from hierarchical data structures like BSTs, AVL, and Red-Black trees in optimizing data organization.

Applications and Techniques

  • Analyzing sorting and searching algorithms and their real-world utility.
  • Differentiating between trees and graphs and their traversal techniques.
  • Graph algorithms: spanning trees and shortest paths for networking and logistics.

Design Principles

Reflections on different design strategies and trade-offs:

  • Balancing constraints while designing efficient algorithms.
  • Breaking down problems into smaller, manageable components.
  • Adapting existing solutions to new challenges and leveraging innovation where needed.
